# NIS2 In Plain Language

NIS2 is a European Union cybersecurity directive. A directive is a legal instrument that EU Member States must transpose into national law.

In simple terms, NIS2 expects covered organizations to manage cybersecurity as an ongoing responsibility rather than a one-time technical project.

## What NIS2 Expects

NIS2 expects organizations to:

- Know which systems and services matter.
- Manage cyber risks.
- Protect systems and networks.
- Detect and handle incidents.
- Report significant incidents when required.
- Train people.
- Manage suppliers and dependencies.
- Keep evidence that security work is actually happening.

## What Evidence Means

Evidence is a file or record that proves a claim. Examples include:

- A risk register.
- A control register.
- A training attendance list.
- A sanitized monitoring summary.
- A vulnerability remediation record.
- A public-safe playbook.
- A hash manifest that fingerprints evidence files.

## What NIS2COMPASS Adds

NIS2COMPASS provides a reusable way to organize NIS2 readiness evidence. It is not a legal opinion and does not replace formal legal or audit advice.
