# CYRESCUE

CYRESCUE focuses on preparedness, incident response, and resilience. In plain language, it asks whether an organization can discover weaknesses safely, practise its response, learn from the exercise, and recover without turning a controlled test into a new risk.

## What This Means For NIS2COMPASS

NIS2COMPASS uses the CYRESCUE role to organize public guidance for authorized validation, Rules of Engagement, reviewable test records, remediation evidence, and lessons learned. Public material describes the method and safety boundaries. It does not publish targets, findings, payloads, credentials, private architecture, or step-by-step attack instructions.

## Independent Open-Source Reference

<!-- tool-ref:apache-caldera -->

**[Apache Caldera](https://caldera.apache.org/)** is an Apache-2.0 adversary-emulation platform that security teams can study for controlled defensive validation. It is listed here as an independent upstream research reference relevant to CYRESCUE's public role. NIS2COMPASS does not present it as a CYberSynchrony-supplied component, a completed integration, or a tool approved for unrestricted use.

Because adversary emulation is dual-use, any real activity requires written authorization, an isolated scope, explicit stop conditions, responsible operators, and review of the resulting evidence.
